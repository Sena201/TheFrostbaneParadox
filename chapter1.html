The Heart That Doesn’t Beat

The city never slept, but neither did it breathe. Neon veins pulsed beneath steel skies...

Sena Frostbane moved like a ghost in the futuristic sprawl, seventeen years old but carrying a weight far beyond his years...

A child screamed somewhere nearby—caught beneath a malfunctioning drone’s arm...

What is happiness? he wondered, staring into the city’s dim glow. A word I have forgotten.

Memories teased the edges of his mind—fragmented flashes from the day the curse took hold...

Tonight, the relic’s echo called to him again.

Beneath the city’s glittering facade, in the shadowed depths where outlaws and outcasts gathered, the Auction of Shadows was about to begin...

When the final item was brought forward—a small obsidian chest inlaid with gold filigree...

"Property of the Forgotten Sea," the auctioneer intoned. "Do not awaken."

Yet, Sena’s hand was already outstretched...

The compass, Kofa, bonded instantly...

His breath caught. Steel and neon vanished.

The sea called.

When Sena opened his eyes, salt stung his lips...

Islands—some wild and ancient, others glowing with future light—rose from the mist.

Sena’s voice was a whisper against the crashing waves:
“This isn’t my time.”
